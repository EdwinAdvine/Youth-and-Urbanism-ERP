# Recruitment & Applicant Tracking

Urban Vibes Dynamics's built-in Applicant Tracking System (ATS) manages the full hiring lifecycle — from raising a job requisition to generating an offer letter and converting the successful candidate into an employee record. No external HR platform needed.

## Creating a Job Requisition

Before advertising a vacancy, an authorised HR officer or department head creates a requisition:

1. Navigate to **HR → Recruitment → New Requisition**.
2. Fill in the requisition details:
   - **Job Title** — e.g., "Senior Accountant" or "Logistics Coordinator".
   - **Department** — the department the role belongs to.
   - **Headcount** — number of positions to fill (usually 1, but bulk hiring is supported).
   - **Employment Type** — Full-time, Part-time, or Contract.
   - **Salary Range (KES)** — minimum and maximum monthly gross (e.g., KES 80,000 – KES 120,000). This is for internal reference and optionally shown on the public listing.
   - **Target Start Date** — when you need the person to start.
   - **Job Description** — full description of responsibilities, qualifications required, and skills. A rich-text editor is available.
3. Click **Submit for Approval**. Depending on your approval policy, the requisition may need sign-off from a department head or Finance (to confirm budget) before the job is published.

## Publishing the Job

Once approved, click **Publish Job**. The role appears on your company's public careers page (auto-generated by Urban Vibes Dynamics at `/careers` on your domain). You can also copy the direct application link to share on LinkedIn, your company website, or local job boards like BrighterMonday Kenya or MyJobMag.

## Managing Candidates Through the Pipeline

All applications flow into the ATS pipeline. The default pipeline stages are:

**Applied → Screening → Interview → Offer → Hired**

Each stage is a Kanban-style column. Candidates appear as cards showing name, application date, and CV summary.

### Moving Candidates Through Stages

1. Open **HR → Recruitment → [Job Title]**.
2. Find the candidate card and drag it to the next stage, or open the card and click **Move to [Stage]**.
3. Add an **Internal Note** visible only to the hiring team (e.g., "Strong Excel skills, weak presentation — worth interviewing").
4. Send a stage update email to the candidate directly from the ATS by clicking **Email Candidate** — pre-built templates are available for each transition (screening invite, interview invite, regret letter, offer letter).

### Screening

Review uploaded CVs and cover letters. Mark candidates as **Shortlisted** or **Rejected**. Rejected candidates can be sent an automated regret email in one click. Shortlisted candidates move to the Interview stage.

### Interview Scheduling

1. Open the candidate card and click **Schedule Interview**.
2. Select the interview type (Phone Screen, Video — links to Jitsi, In-person).
3. Pick a date and time from the calendar. The system checks the interviewer's calendar for conflicts.
4. The candidate receives an automated email with interview details and (for video) a Jitsi meeting link.
5. After the interview, the interviewer submits a structured **Interview Scorecard** — rating the candidate on pre-defined criteria (Technical Skills, Culture Fit, Communication, etc.) on a 1–5 scale.

### Making an Offer

1. Move the candidate to **Offer** stage.
2. Click **Generate Offer Letter**.
3. The system populates a pre-built offer letter template with the candidate's name, job title, salary (KES), start date, and reporting line.
4. Review and customise the letter, then click **Send Offer**. The candidate receives the offer letter by email with an acceptance link.
5. Once the candidate accepts, their status updates to **Offer Accepted**.

## Converting a Hire to an Employee Record

When a candidate's stage is set to **Hired** (after offer acceptance):

1. Click **Create Employee Record**.
2. The system pre-fills the New Employee form with the candidate's personal details, job title, department, salary, and start date sourced from the ATS.
3. Review, add any missing fields (bank account, KRA PIN, etc.), and click **Save Employee**.
4. The new employee record is live and ready for onboarding — attendance, leave, and payroll are all immediately active.

The ATS record is archived and linked to the employee profile for future reference.

> **Tip:** Close unfilled or cancelled requisitions promptly. Open requisitions with no activity appear in the HR dashboard's outstanding items list, making it harder to track genuine active hiring.
