# Performance Reviews & Goals

Urban Vibes Dynamics's performance module provides a structured way to set goals, conduct periodic reviews, and document development plans — replacing ad-hoc annual chats with a consistent, trackable process.

## Navigating to Performance

Go to **HR → Performance**. The page has three tabs: **Goals**, **Reviews**, and **Development Plans**. Both managers and employees interact with this module; what each party can see and edit is controlled by their role.

## Setting Goals

Goals in Urban Vibes Dynamics follow the **SMART format**: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. A well-written goal has a clear metric and a deadline.

### Creating a Goal

1. Go to **HR → Performance → Goals → New Goal**.
2. Select the **Employee** the goal is for.
3. Fill in:
   - **Goal Title** — short and clear (e.g., "Increase client retention rate").
   - **Description** — detailed explanation of what success looks like.
   - **Key Result / Metric** — the measurable outcome (e.g., "Retention rate from 72% to 85% by December 2026").
   - **Category** — choose from: Individual, Team, or Organisational.
   - **Due Date** — the target completion date.
   - **Weight (%)** — if the employee has multiple goals, weights determine each goal's contribution to the overall performance score. Weights must total 100%.
4. Click **Save Goal**.

The employee receives a notification that a goal has been assigned to them. They can view all their goals under **HR → Performance → My Goals**.

### Tracking Progress

Throughout the review period, employees update their goal progress:

1. Open the goal and click **Update Progress**.
2. Enter the current value of the metric and a brief comment on status.
3. Mark status as: **On Track**, **At Risk**, or **Off Track**.

Managers see a progress dashboard for their direct reports, letting them intervene early if a goal is off track.

## Self-Assessment

Before a formal review, employees complete a self-assessment:

1. Navigate to **HR → Performance → Reviews → [Review Cycle]**.
2. Find your name and click **Start Self-Assessment**.
3. For each competency (e.g., Communication, Execution, Teamwork, Initiative), select a rating from **1 (Poor) to 5 (Outstanding)** and write a supporting comment.
4. Write a summary paragraph of your achievements and challenges for the period.
5. Click **Submit Self-Assessment**.

Self-assessments are visible to the line manager once submitted.

## Manager Review

After the employee submits their self-assessment, the manager conducts the formal review:

1. Go to **HR → Performance → Reviews → Approval Queue**.
2. Click the employee's name to open their review.
3. Rate each competency (1–5) with supporting comments. The employee's self-rating is shown alongside for reference.
4. Review goal completion: the system auto-calculates how many goals were met, partially met, or missed.
5. The system computes an **Overall Performance Score** as a weighted average of competency ratings and goal attainment.
6. Add a summary narrative and any key observations.
7. Click **Submit Review**.

The employee is notified and can view the completed review (but cannot edit it after the manager submits).

## Overall Rating Scale

| Score | Rating Label |
|---|---|
| 4.5 – 5.0 | Outstanding |
| 3.5 – 4.4 | Exceeds Expectations |
| 2.5 – 3.4 | Meets Expectations |
| 1.5 – 2.4 | Needs Improvement |
| 1.0 – 1.4 | Unsatisfactory |

## Development Plans

Following a review, the manager and employee agree on a development plan:

1. Go to the completed review and click **Add Development Plan**.
2. List specific actions (e.g., "Enrol in Finance for Non-Finance Managers course on LMS by April 2026").
3. Set a target date and assign accountability (employee or manager).
4. Development plan items are tracked in **HR → Performance → Development Plans** with a completion checkbox.

## Annual Review Cycle & Salary Increments

HR Admins configure review cycles under **HR → Settings → Review Cycles** (e.g., Annual: January–December, with reviews in January of the following year; or Semi-Annual: two review windows per year).

Performance scores from the annual review feed into the salary increment process. HR Admins can run a **Salary Increment Report** under **HR → Reports → Compensation** that lists each employee, their performance rating, current salary, and a recommended increment range (configurable bands, e.g., Outstanding = 10–15%, Meets Expectations = 3–5%).

The final increment decision remains with HR management, but the system provides the data needed to make it consistently and fairly.

> **Tip:** Goal-setting works best when done collaboratively. Encourage managers to involve employees in writing their own goals — employees who help define their targets are more committed to achieving them.
