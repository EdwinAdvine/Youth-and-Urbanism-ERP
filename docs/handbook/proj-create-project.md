# Creating & Managing Projects

Urban Vibes Dynamics's Projects module gives your team a shared workspace to plan, track, and deliver work — with Kanban boards, task assignments, time logging, and direct links to Finance, CRM, Drive, and Mail.

## Creating a New Project

1. Go to **Projects** in the left sidebar.
2. Click **New Project** (top-right button).
3. Fill in the project details:
   - **Project Name** — clear and descriptive (e.g., "Westlands Office Fit-Out Q2 2026").
   - **Description** — a brief summary of the project's scope and objectives.
   - **Client** — link to a CRM contact or company. Once linked, the project appears on the client's CRM profile and any associated deal value is visible on the project dashboard.
   - **Start Date** and **End Date** — the planned project duration. Tasks with due dates outside this range are flagged with a warning.
   - **Budget (KES)** — the approved project budget. As expenses and logged time (with billable rates) accumulate, a budget burn bar tracks how much has been consumed.
   - **Status** — set to "Planning" initially; changes to "Active", "On Hold", or "Completed" as the project progresses.
   - **Visibility** — Private (only project members can see it) or Internal (visible to all logged-in users on the account).
4. Click **Save Project**.

## Adding Team Members

On the project page, go to the **Team** tab:

1. Click **Add Member**.
2. Search for an employee by name.
3. Assign their **Role on Project**: Project Manager, Developer, Designer, Analyst, or a custom role.
4. Set their **Billable Rate (KES/hr)** if time tracking is used for client billing.
5. Click **Add**.

Team members receive an email notification that they have been added to the project. The project now appears in their **Projects → My Projects** list.

The **Project Manager** role gets additional permissions: editing project details, approving time logs, and closing the project.

## The Kanban Board

The Board view is the main working surface for your team. Click the **Board** tab on any project.

Default columns are:

- **To Do** — work not yet started.
- **In Progress** — actively being worked on.
- **Review** — completed work awaiting review or approval.
- **Done** — finished and accepted.

HR Admins or Project Managers can add custom columns or rename existing ones (e.g., add a "Blocked" column) by clicking the **+** icon at the right edge of the board.

## Creating Tasks

1. Click **+ Add Task** at the top of any column (or the **+ New Task** button above the board).
2. Fill in the task card:
   - **Task Title** — concise action statement (e.g., "Design electrical layout for Floor 3").
   - **Assignee** — select a team member from the project's member list.
   - **Due Date** — the deadline for this task.
   - **Priority** — Low, Medium, High, or Urgent. High and Urgent tasks are highlighted in red on the board.
   - **Labels / Tags** — freeform tags to group related tasks (e.g., "electrical", "phase-1", "client-facing").
   - **Description** — detailed instructions, acceptance criteria, or reference links.
3. Click **Save Task**.

The task card appears in the selected column. The assignee receives an in-app and email notification.

## Managing Tasks

**Open a task card** by clicking it on the board. Inside you can:

- **Edit** any field (title, assignee, dates, priority).
- **Add Comments** — @mention teammates to bring them into the conversation.
- **Attach Files** — drag-and-drop or browse. Files are stored in the project's linked Drive folder.
- **Log Time** — click Log Time to record hours worked (see the Time Tracking article for details).
- **View Activity Log** — a full history of all changes to the task.

**Moving tasks between columns**: drag the card to a different column or open the task and change the **Status** dropdown.

## Tracking Project Progress

The **Overview** tab on the project shows:

- **Progress Bar** — percentage of tasks in "Done" status.
- **Budget Burn** — KES spent (logged time at billable rates + linked Finance expenses) vs. the project budget.
- **Upcoming Deadlines** — tasks due in the next 7 days.
- **Team Workload** — tasks per assignee, useful for spotting overloaded team members.

When all tasks are complete and the client has signed off, set the project status to **Completed**. A completion date is recorded and the project moves to the archived list.

> **Tip:** Keep task titles action-oriented and specific. "Website" is a category, not a task. "Write homepage copy — 500 words" is a task. Specific tasks are easier to assign, estimate, and mark as truly done.
