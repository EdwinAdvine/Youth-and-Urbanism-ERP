# Knowledge Base Articles

A well-maintained knowledge base reduces the number of repetitive tickets your team has to handle. Customers find answers themselves; agents spend less time on common questions. Urban Vibes Dynamics's built-in Knowledge Base module makes it straightforward to create, publish, and continuously improve your support content.

## Creating an Article

1. Go to **Support → Knowledge Base**.
2. Click **New Article**.
3. Fill in the article details:
   - **Title**: clear and searchable (e.g., "How to Reset Your Password" or "Understanding Your Invoice")
   - **Content**: use the rich text editor to write the article body. Add headings, numbered steps, screenshots, and code blocks as needed.
   - **Category**: select or create a category (e.g., "Billing", "Account Setup", "Technical Issues"). Categories appear as sections on your customer-facing portal.
   - **Tags**: add tags to improve search relevance (e.g., `password`, `login`, `access`).
4. Click **Save Draft** to keep it unpublished while you refine it, or **Publish** to make it live immediately.

## Visibility: Public vs. Internal

Each article has a visibility setting:

- **Public** — the article appears on the customer self-service portal. Customers can find it via search without logging in. Use this for FAQs, how-to guides, and troubleshooting steps that any customer might need.
- **Internal** — visible only to your Support agents when they are working on tickets. Use this for internal escalation procedures, refund policies, or agent training notes that should not be customer-facing.

You can change an article's visibility at any time by clicking **Edit** and updating the **Visibility** toggle.

## AI Article Suggestions for Agents

When an agent types a reply on a ticket, Urban Bad AI analyses the ticket content and automatically suggests relevant knowledge base articles in a sidebar panel. The agent can:

- Click **Insert Link** to embed the KB article link directly into their reply.
- Click **Copy Summary** to paste a condensed version of the article into the reply text.

This dramatically speeds up first-response times on common issues — the agent does not need to search manually.

## Article Helpfulness Feedback

At the bottom of every public knowledge base article, customers see a feedback prompt:

> **Was this article helpful?** 👍 Yes   👎 No

Feedback is recorded and displayed in the article's analytics panel (visible to agents and admins). Articles with consistently low helpfulness scores are flagged for review. If a customer clicks 👎, they are optionally prompted to describe what was missing — this feedback appears as a note on the article for editors.

## Linking KB Articles in Ticket Replies

To insert a knowledge base article link while replying to a ticket:

1. In the reply editor, click the **KB** icon in the toolbar (or type `/kb` as a slash command).
2. A search panel opens — type keywords to find relevant articles.
3. Select the article. The link is inserted into your reply automatically.

When you insert a KB link, Urban Vibes Dynamics also updates the article's **Usage in Tickets** counter — useful for tracking which articles are most frequently referenced by agents.

## Managing the Knowledge Base

From **Support → Knowledge Base → Manage**, you can:

- **Reorder articles** within a category by dragging.
- **Archive** outdated articles (they are removed from the portal but kept in the system for reference).
- **View analytics** per article: views, searches that led to it, helpfulness score, and tickets where it was linked.

Aim to review articles at least quarterly. Outdated instructions are worse than no article — customers lose trust when the steps described do not match what they see in the product.
