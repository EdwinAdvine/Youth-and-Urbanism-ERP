# Kenya Payroll FAQs

These are the most common questions HR officers and finance teams ask when setting up or running payroll in Urban Vibes Dynamics for a Kenya-registered business.

---

## How is PAYE calculated?

PAYE (Pay As You Earn) is calculated on an employee's taxable income using tiered rates set by the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA):

| Monthly Taxable Income (KES) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 24,000 | 10% |
| 24,001 – 32,333 | 25% |
| Above 32,333 | 30% |

After applying the rates, KRA grants every resident employee a **personal relief of KES 2,400 per month**, which is deducted directly from the gross PAYE calculated. So an employee with taxable income of KES 50,000 would have tax computed across all three bands, then KES 2,400 subtracted.

Urban Vibes Dynamics applies these brackets automatically. If KRA announces a change to the tax bands, your Super Admin can update them under **HR → Settings → Tax Brackets** without waiting for a system update.

---

## What is the NHIF contribution rate?

NHIF (National Hospital Insurance Fund) contributions follow a sliding scale based on gross salary. The rate is not a flat percentage — it is a fixed amount per income band as published by NHIF. For example, an employee earning KES 50,000 gross falls into a specific band and pays the corresponding monthly contribution.

The employer does not automatically match the employee's NHIF contribution under the standard NHIF Act, but some organisations offer a supplementary medical top-up — that would be configured as a separate allowance/deduction rather than a statutory line.

Urban Vibes Dynamics stores the current NHIF rate table and applies the correct deduction based on each employee's gross. The HR Admin can view and update the table under **HR → Settings → NHIF Rates** if NHIF revises the bands.

---

## What is the difference between NSSF Tier I and Tier II?

Under the NSSF Act 2013, contributions are split into two tiers:

- **Tier I**: Applies to earnings up to the Lower Earnings Limit (LEL). The employee contributes 6% of the LEL and the employer matches it. This is capped and the same for virtually all employees.
- **Tier II**: Applies to pensionable earnings between the LEL and the Upper Earnings Limit (UEL). Again, 6% employee and 6% employer. Higher earners contribute more in Tier II.

On payslips generated by Urban Vibes Dynamics, you will see separate line items for "NSSF Tier I (Employee)" and "NSSF Tier II (Employee)" and corresponding employer cost lines in the payroll summary. The NSSF schedule report (exportable from HR → Payroll → [Run] → Exports) is formatted for direct submission to the NSSF portal.

---

## What is the Housing Levy and who pays it?

The Affordable Housing Levy was introduced under the Affordable Housing Act. It requires:

- **Employee contribution**: 1.5% of gross monthly salary.
- **Employer contribution**: 1.5% of the employee's gross monthly salary (an additional employer cost).

Both contributions are remitted to the Kenya Revenue Authority on behalf of the National Housing Corporation. Urban Vibes Dynamics calculates both the employee deduction and the employer obligation automatically for every employee in a payroll run. The combined employer and employee amounts are shown in the payroll run summary and reflected in the journal entry posted to Finance.

## When must payroll be submitted to KRA and other statutory bodies?

| Obligation | Deadline | Portal |
|---|---|---|
| PAYE remittance | 9th of the following month | KRA iTax |
| NHIF remittance | 9th of the following month | NHIF Employer Portal |
| NSSF remittance | 15th of the following month | NSSF Employer Portal |
| Housing Levy | 9th of the following month | KRA iTax |

Urban Vibes Dynamics generates the export files you need for each of these: the PAYE P9 schedule for iTax, an NHIF schedule CSV, and an NSSF schedule CSV. These are available under **HR → Payroll → [Run] → Exports** once the run is approved.

## Can I run a mid-month or off-cycle payroll?

Yes. When creating a payroll run, the Pay Period field is free to set to any date range, not just a full calendar month. A mid-month run is useful for:

- Paying a consultant or contract worker who invoices mid-cycle.
- Processing a departing employee's final settlement before the main payroll date.
- Paying a bonus or commission outside the regular cycle.

Select the specific employee group (or individual employees) rather than "All Employees" for an off-cycle run. The system calculates prorations and statutory deductions based on the number of days in the pay period.

---

## How do I handle an employee who joins mid-month?

Urban Vibes Dynamics prorates the salary automatically. When an employee's start date is set mid-month (e.g., 15th March), the payroll engine calculates:

`Prorated Gross = (Monthly Gross ÷ Total Working Days in March) × Working Days from Start Date to End of Month`

Statutory deductions (PAYE, NHIF, NSSF, Housing Levy) are then applied to the prorated gross, not the full monthly gross. The payslip clearly shows "Prorated: X days of Y" so the employee understands the calculation.

## How are loan repayments or salary advances handled?

Deductions such as salary advances, SACCO loan repayments, or company loan repayments are set up as **Custom Deductions** on the employee record (HR → Employees → [Employee] → Deductions tab). Enter the description, monthly deduction amount, and (optionally) a total loan balance so the system can track how much remains.

Custom deductions are applied after all statutory deductions and reduce the net pay. They appear as their own line item on the payslip. Once the total balance is recovered, the deduction automatically stops — no manual action needed.
